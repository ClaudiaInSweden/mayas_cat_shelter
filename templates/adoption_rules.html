Adoption Rules and Process

Things to consider before adopting a cat

To adopt a cat from a cat shelter involves a lot of responsibility. Cats who come to our cat shelter have often been homeless for some reasons, or need a new home due to illness or death of their previous owners. We don't want our cats to go through such a traumatic experience again so we are very carefully in chosing a new, forever home.

Questions you need to ask yourself:

  • Does your financial situation allow for unexpected expenditures, like acute veterinary care which can be expensive even with an insurance?
  • Can you afford high-quality food, litter and insurance even in a couple of years?
  • Do you have enough time for a cat?
  • Is there a risk that you need to move to a place where pets are not allowed?
  • Do you have somebody who can take care of the cat if you are sick or when you travel?
Adoption fee and common information

Adoption fee for a cat is 2000 SEK.

The cat will be:

  • Spayed or neutered *see also Adoption of kittens
  • Microchipped and registered
  • Vaccinated and dewormed
  • Veterinary checked
  • Insurance policy can be taken over when adopting a cat

Adoption of kittens:

  • Kittens are not adopted out before they are 14 weeks old.
  • We do not allow adoptions of single kittens as kittens do need playmates. Exceptions are possible if you do have a younger cat at home already.
  • The adopter commits to spay/neuter the kitten before it reaches the age of 6 months. We require evidence that this has been done (e.g. vet bills) and reserve the right to take the kitten back if there is a breach of contract.
Steps to adoption

  • Step 1:

    Check out our cats and fill in the adoption form on our website. Up to three cats may be selected.

  • Step 2:

    We contact you by telephone and invite you to visit us and the cat you want to adopt.

  • Step 3:

    During your visit we will go through the adoption contract with you and ask additional questions about your living situation. We will agree on a time for a home visit to meet your family and your other pets and to check out if your housing situation suits the selected cat.

  • Step 4:

    After the home visit and when we approve your adoption request, you are required to visit your cat at least three more times within a 14 days period. We encourage you to leave some clothes or similar items so that the cat can get familiar to your smell.

  • Step 5:

    On the agreed date and time you come to pick up your cat. You sign the adoption contract and pay the adoption fee. We also recommend to update the registration data while at our office.

    You will get access to our private Facebook group where you can ask questions and connect with other adopters. We also encourage you to use the group to post updates, just because we love to hear from our cats.
